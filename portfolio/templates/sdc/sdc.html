Self-Driving car

About the project

        The initial phase of my project focused on designing and implementing a Level 3 autonomous vehicle in the Carla Simulator. The showcased video demonstrates the capabilities of this first version. Beyond typical driving conditions, the system adeptly handles a variety of tasks and scenarios. These include maintaining lane stability, executing overtaking maneuvers, navigating around roadblocks, and responding to jaywalking incidents. Integrated within the solution are concepts from deep learning, image processing, state estimation, localization, and motion planning. Moreover, the project introduces robust strategies for addressing sensor failures and pioneers an innovative approach to knowledge sharing. This endeavor was the capstone of my undergraduate studies.

        The latest iteration seeks to integrate additional onboard sensors, such as LIDAR and RADAR, to bolster localization, self-awareness, and maneuverability. Additionally, it will demonstrate how insights (or experiences) from other autonomous entities in the environment can greatly enhance planning and decision-making, leveraging imitation learning to expedite the learning curve. The project will also involve designing dynamic models to predict the movements of moving objects and to adapt to a range of weather conditions. Ultimately, the goal is to advance to a Level 4 autonomous vehicle. This is at its rudimentary phase and will be updated as the project progresses. More details can be found in my github repo.
